Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know before you book.

How do I book an appointment?

+

Book directly through our Vagaro page at vagaro.com/redemptiontenfold, message us on Facebook, call (906) 792-6001, or email Tenfoldtattoo@gmail.com. We recommend booking as early as possible — our artists' schedules fill quickly and we want to give every project the time it deserves.

Do you take walk-ins?

+

Walk-in availability varies day to day. For the best experience — and to ensure you get the artist and the time your piece needs — we strongly recommend booking in advance. Check our Vagaro page or reach out to see what's available.

What styles do you specialize in?

+

Redemption TenFold covers the full range: Black & Grey Realism, Illustrative Portraiture, Blackwork, Color, and Fine Line. Our artists have earned national recognition — magazine publications, sponsored pro team placements, and competition wins. Whatever you're after, we have someone who's made it their craft.

How much does a tattoo cost?

+

Pricing depends on size, complexity, placement, and the artist. Reach out with your idea and we'll give you an honest estimate before you commit. Quality work is never the cheapest option — but it's always the most worth it.

Is a deposit required?

+

Yes, a deposit is required to hold your appointment. It applies toward the final cost of your tattoo. The deposit is non-refundable for cancellations but transferable to a rescheduled appointment with adequate notice. Contact us directly for specifics on your booking.

Can I bring reference photos?

+

Absolutely — reference photos help a lot. You don't need a finished concept; even a feeling, a mood, or an image that resonates gives your artist a direction to work from. Our artists don't copy other tattoos, but references establish scale, tone, and intention.

What should I do to prepare for my session?

+

Sleep well the night before. Eat a full meal 2–3 hours prior. Stay hydrated in the days leading up — not just the morning of. Wear clothing that allows easy access to the area being tattooed. Avoid alcohol for 24 hours beforehand. Don't apply numbing cream without checking with your artist first.

Post-Session Care

Protecting your investment

A great tattoo is a collaboration that continues after you leave the studio. Follow these steps carefully — how you care for the work in the first two weeks determines how it looks for the rest of your life.

01

First 24 Hours

Leave the bandage or wrap your artist applied in place for 2–4 hours, or up to 24 hours if a Saniderm / second-skin bandage was used. Remove gently under lukewarm running water. Wash with fragrance-free soap using clean hands only — no washcloths or sponges. Pat dry with a clean paper towel. Apply a thin layer of unscented lotion or your artist's recommended ointment.

02

Days 2 – 14

Wash twice daily and apply a thin layer of unscented moisturizer each time. The tattoo will peel and flake — this is normal. Do not pick, scratch, or peel the skin. Wear loose clothing over the area. Avoid submerging the tattoo in water (no baths, pools, hot tubs, or open water). Keep it out of direct sunlight. Minimize heavy sweating over the area when possible.

03

Long-Term Care

Once fully healed (typically 3–4 weeks), apply SPF 50+ sunscreen whenever the tattoo will be in the sun. UV exposure is the number one cause of fading. Keep your skin moisturized year-round — hydrated skin holds ink better over time. Avoid prolonged exposure to chlorine or saltwater. Schedule a touch-up 8–10 weeks out if needed.

Watch for these warning signs: Some redness and minor swelling in the first 48 hours is normal. If you notice excessive swelling, discharge, a spreading rash, or fever, contact a medical professional — these are signs of infection, not just healing. Don't hesitate to reach us at Tenfoldtattoo@gmail.com or (906) 792-6001 if you have any questions about your healing.
