FREQUENTLY ASKED

Everything you need before you book.

How do I book an appointment?

+

You can book directly through our Vagaro page at vagaro.com/redemptiontenfold, or reach us by messaging our Facebook page. You can also call the studio at (906) 792-6001 or email us at Tenfoldtattoo@gmail.com. We recommend booking as early as possible — our artists' schedules move fast.

Do you take walk-ins?

+

We occasionally have walk-in availability, but we can't guarantee it. For the best experience — and to ensure you get the artist you want — we strongly recommend booking in advance. Message us on Facebook or check our Vagaro page to see current availability.

What styles do your artists specialize in?

+

Redemption TenFold covers the full range: Black & Grey Realism, Illustrative Portraiture, Blackwork, Color, and Fine Line. Our artists have earned national recognition for their work — including magazine publications, sponsored pro team placements, and competition wins. Whatever style you're after, we have someone who's made it their specialty.

How much does a tattoo cost?

+

Pricing depends on the size, complexity, placement, and which artist you're working with. Contact us with your idea and we'll give you an honest estimate before you commit to anything. We'd rather have that conversation upfront than leave you guessing. Quality work is never the cheapest option — but it's always the most worth it.

Is a deposit required?

+

Yes, a deposit is required to hold your appointment. It is applied toward the final cost of your tattoo. The deposit is non-refundable if you cancel without sufficient notice, but is transferable to a rescheduled appointment with adequate advance notice. Contact us directly for specifics on your booking.

Where exactly are you located?

+

We're at 1112 10th Street, Menominee, Michigan 49858. Menominee sits right on the Wisconsin border near Marinette — accessible from Green Bay, Escanaba, and the entire Upper Peninsula. If you're making the drive, reach out and we'll make it worth it.

What should I do to prepare for my session?

+

Sleep well the night before. Eat a full meal 2–3 hours before your appointment. Stay well-hydrated in the days leading up to your session. Wear or bring clothing that allows easy access to the area being tattooed. Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours prior. Don't apply numbing cream without consulting your artist first — it can affect the skin's texture.

Post-Session Care

PROTECT YOUR INVESTMENT

01

First 24 Hours

Leave the bandage or wrap your artist applied in place for 2–4 hours (or up to 24 hours if a Saniderm / second-skin bandage was used). When removing, do so gently under lukewarm running water. Wash with fragrance-free soap using only clean hands — no washcloths or loofahs. Pat dry with a clean paper towel. Apply a thin layer of unscented lotion or your artist's recommended ointment.

02

Days 2 – 14

Wash twice daily and apply a thin layer of unscented moisturizer after each wash. Your tattoo will peel and flake — this is completely normal. Do not pick, scratch, or peel the skin. Wear loose clothing over the area. Avoid submerging the tattoo in water (no baths, pools, hot tubs, or open water). Keep it out of direct sunlight. Avoid heavy sweating over the area when possible.

03

Long-Term

Once healed (typically 3–4 weeks), apply SPF 50+ sunscreen any time the tattoo is exposed to sun. UV is the number one cause of fading. Keep your skin moisturized year-round — hydrated skin holds ink better over time. Avoid prolonged exposure to chlorine or saltwater. Schedule a touch-up 8–10 weeks after your session if needed.

Watch for these warning signs: Some redness and minor swelling in the first 48 hours is normal. If you experience excessive swelling, discharge, a spreading rash, or fever, seek medical attention immediately — these are signs of infection, not just healing. Don't hesitate to reach out to us at Tenfoldtattoo@gmail.com or call (906) 792-6001 with any questions about your healing.
